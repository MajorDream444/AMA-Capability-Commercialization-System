# Commercialization Doctrine v1

The operating philosophy for how AMA Solutions takes the Hanzo sovereign operating system, the Enso intelligence-selection layer, and the wider Lux ecosystem to market. This doctrine governs every offer, ICP, proposal, and delivery playbook. Where it conflicts with a specific document, this doctrine and the `claim-governance.md` rules win.

> Canonical spellings: Hanzo, Enso, Lux, AMA Solutions, Zach Kelling, MAIM.

## 1. The north star: valuable even when the answer is "no"

AMA is a **capability architect**, not a product-pusher. The diagnostic that opens most engagements must remain valuable **even when it concludes "there is no responsible Enso pilot here."** A legitimate, successful outcome of an AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic is: *"Your spend is already well-optimized; no economically meaningful pilot is warranted."*

This is not a soft principle — it is the source of AMA's credibility and pricing power. A firm that will tell a prospect the honest answer, including the unprofitable one, earns the trust that later justifies elephant- and whale-scale engagements. A firm that steers every assessment toward the same product becomes a vendor, and is priced like one. This principle is written into `offer-spec.md` and `success-metrics.md` in the delivery repository and may not be diluted by conversion incentives.

## 2. Rabbits first

AMA leads with **rabbits**: small, fast, tightly-scoped entry offers that solve an immediate, measurable pain and require no act of faith in the full vertical.

- **Rabbit** — high-velocity, quantifiable value, short sales cycle, standardized delivery, minimal founder dependence. The AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic is the flagship rabbit.
- **Elephant** — core operational modernization (engineering modernization, organizational intelligence platform); higher trust, longer cycle, larger scope.
- **Whale** — sovereign / regulated / mission-critical infrastructure; long procurement, evidence-heavy, bespoke.

A whale-caliber account may *enter through a rabbit*. Always distinguish **account potential** (could become a whale) from **initial offer size** (a rabbit) from **current opportunity stage** (discovery). Never try to sell the whale on day one; never treat a rabbit as merely small.

Rationale grounded in source material: the Sovereign OS Blueprint and both podcast pressure-tests converge on the same point — prospects will not grant deep infrastructure access to an unproven vendor, but they will buy a bounded, read-only, dollar-denominated audit. The rabbit is the trust-building transaction that unlocks the vertical.

## 3. Lead with the outcome, not the stack

Do not open with post-quantum cryptography, FHE, 700 repositories, 400 models, or blockchain. The full vertical is the **expansion architecture**, not the first sales sentence. Lead with the immediate business outcome the buyer already wants:

- reduce and make visible AI + cloud spend;
- organize scattered institutional knowledge;
- expose unsafe or ungoverned AI usage;
- improve engineering throughput.

The cognitive load of the full stack is a wall of friction; a bounded outcome is a door. Once inside, the vertical becomes the expansion path.

## 4. Avoid the agency trap

The single largest structural risk is becoming a low-margin, high-stress bespoke dev shop where every engagement requires Zach Kelling to write custom code. If delivery cannot be executed from a documented playbook, the path to scale collapses under its own support burden.

Doctrine:
- Every rabbit must run on the **pre-configured vertical stack**, not on bespoke engineering.
- Delivery is designed for **repeatable analyst- or architect-led execution after the internal dry run, playbook validation, and escalation protocol are complete.** (This is a design goal reached through validation — not a claim of current readiness. See `zach-escalation-policy.md` when it lands.)
- Standardization is a deliverable, not an afterthought. If a rabbit cannot be productized, it is an elephant and must be priced and staffed as one.

## 5. Blended acquisition, not bespoke-only

Reaching a large paying base cannot be achieved through bespoke high-touch sales alone — the headcount math fails. AMA uses a blended model across high-touch engagements, standardized assisted deployments, channel/partner-delivered customers, and product-led self-serve. The exact cohort mix and per-cohort support model are defined in `customer-cohort-definitions.md` (PR #3) and remain subject to DR-1. "First 1,000 customers" is a **scale architecture, not a guaranteed 90-day acquisition promise** (Decision 12).

## 6. Claim discipline is a sales asset

Over-claiming is the fastest way to lose an enterprise procurement conversation. AMA sells only the strongest **defensible** version of any claim, per `claim-governance.md`:

- No "number-one AI in the world," guaranteed savings percentages, benchmark supremacy, fully autonomous companies, or absolute privacy without deployment-specific proof.
- Company-published material (the Compendium) establishes what Hanzo *officially states* — it is provenance, not independent verification, and never sets evidence status.
- The reproducible-evidence gap is itself a roadmap: the Enso evidence package (DR-8) and case-study permission (DR-7) are the artifacts that convert today's "Founder-Reported" into tomorrow's "Verified." Until then, AMA sells the diagnostic, not the claims.

## 7. Human governance is non-negotiable

Every agent-operated company is marketed as **operating under human governance** (Decision 10). AMA does not sell, imply, or build legally autonomous AI entities. A human founder holds beneficial ownership, passes KYC/KYB, sets spending limits, and bears legal and tax accountability. Prospects seeking a liability-free autonomous shell are disqualified. This boundary protects AMA, Hanzo, and the client, and is reflected in every offer's disqualification rules.

## 8. Sovereignty as the defensible moat — sold honestly

Hanzo's durable differentiation is **deployment and control**, not a claim of superior model quality. The Compendium itself is explicit: *"This is not a claim that our model is smarter than Claude."* The differentiators AMA may assert (as positioning, with provenance) are owned model weights, self-hostable and air-gappable deployment, post-quantum and FHE-native security, and auditability — the things a metered closed cloud cannot offer by construction. The tension between Enso's reported continuous retraining and the sovereignty promise is unresolved (OPEN-QUESTIONS D6) and blocks any Enso-pilot sale until Zach resolves it in writing.

## 9. Protect the scarce resources

Two resources are scarce and must be protected: **Zach's engineering time** and **AMA's focus**. Escalate to Zach only when an opportunity is commercially qualified, has budget and authority, and where proprietary capability materially affects the sale (detailed in `zach-escalation-policy.md` (forthcoming, PR #3)). Protect focus by maintaining a disciplined kill/delay list in `offer-portfolio.md`: strong offers are promoted, overlapping offers combined, weak offers eliminated, and offers dependent on unfinished capability delayed.

## 10. Standard work, every engagement

Gather source material → separate verified facts from claims → define the specific objective → select one ICP and one rabbit → identify triggering events → qualify → conduct founder-informed discovery → record objections → refine the offer → close a bounded pilot → deliver from a documented playbook → measure → build a case study → convert delivery into a repeatable package → expand responsibly. Discipline over improvisation; evidence over hype; bounded proof over grand vision.
