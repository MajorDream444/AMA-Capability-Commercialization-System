## 2. The flagship: two-contract structure (DR-3, resolved)

The AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic is sold as **two separate contracts**, never collapsed into one price and never presented as a guaranteed outcome:

- **Phase A — AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic: $12,000–$20,000.** Establishes a reliable baseline, identifies and categorizes cost drivers, documents assumptions and data limitations, distinguishes confirmed waste from possible optimization, and determines whether a Phase B pilot is justified. **Phase A succeeds even when the honest answer is "no responsible Enso pilot here"** (doctrine §1). Metadata-only, read-only access; no prompt payloads by default.
- **Phase B — Optional Enso Evaluation Pilot: $8,000–$15,000.** Only when Phase A justifies it. May require a controlled, consented, sanitized evaluation sample to measure quality; its data handling is defined in the delivery module's `security-and-access.md`. **Blocked until Zach resolves the Enso data-governance question in writing** (OPEN-QUESTIONS D6).

No savings percentage is marketed as a promise (Decision 7; claim-governance §3). The success criteria live in the delivery repo's `success-metrics.md`.

## 3. Priority ranking

Working priority for the current push, judged on urgency, measurable ROI, sales-cycle length, delivery difficulty, Hanzo readiness, Zach-independence, and expansion potential:

1. **AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic** — highest urgency, clearest dollar-denominated ROI, shortest cycle, read-only access, lowest founder dependence. The Trojan-horse entry to the vertical.
2. **Private Knowledge Vault / Firm Intelligence** — high pain for professional-services and family-office ICPs; softer ROI (time saved, errors prevented) but strong expansion into an organizational-intelligence platform.
3. **AI Governance & Data Exposure Audit** — rising urgency as ungoverned AI usage becomes a board-level risk; pairs naturally with the cost diagnostic.
4. **Agentic Engineering Readiness Sprint** — highest technical value but a slow-burn, trust-heavy sale; positioned as an elephant wedge after trust is established, never as a fast rabbit.

This ranking is not permanent — discovery evidence promotes, combines, narrows, or delays offers.

## 4. Kill / delay list (focus discipline)

Preserving focus means refusing work. Current disposition, grounded in the pressure-test analysis:

- **Delayed — Edge / offline local knowledge customization.** Creeps immediately into whale territory (physical hardware, bespoke local environments, high Zach dependence). Delay until the cloud product is bulletproof and the first cohort is delivered.
- **Delayed — CI/CD test-suite modernization as a *rabbit*.** The capability is real (see the regulated-market case study, Hanzo-Published, DR-7 for naming), but asking an unvetted vendor for CI/CD and core-repo access on a first engagement is an unacceptable risk profile for the buyer. It belongs in the **elephant** tier after trust, not as a fast entry offer.
- **Reframed — "Agentic Company Launch."** The autonomous-entity framing is killed (illegal; violates human-governance, Decision 10). Any survivor is a **Private Client Intelligence Workspace under human governance**, and it remains excluded from the active-4 pending DR-2. Note the contradiction with the Blueprint's "Three Undeniable Entry Offers" is preserved in the decision register (C-02), unresolved.
- **Background — Lux / settlement, satellites, patent enforcement, national infrastructure.** Strategic context, not the first go-to-market motion (doctrine §3).

## 5. Expansion architecture

Every rabbit is designed to expand: cost diagnostic → production Enso routing + platform usage (elephant); knowledge vault → organizational-intelligence platform (elephant); governance audit → ongoing AI-use register and remediation; engineering sprint → full modernization (elephant/whale). A whale-caliber account may enter through any rabbit; account potential, initial offer size, and current stage are always tracked separately (doctrine §2).

## 6. Claim discipline in every offer

No offer, one-pager, or proposal may state a barred claim (claim-governance §3): no "number-one AI," no guaranteed savings percentage, no benchmark supremacy, no fully autonomous company, no absolute privacy without deployment-specific proof. Enso is sold as the intelligent transmission and the control it gives — not a benchmark. Case-study figures are usable only after client permission (DR-7). Every material claim in a sales asset must trace to an entry in `evidence-registry.md`.
