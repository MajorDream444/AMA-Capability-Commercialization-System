# Zach Escalation Policy

When Zach Kelling's engineering time is engaged, and — more often — when it is not. Zach's time is one of two scarce resources (with AMA's focus); protecting it is what keeps AMA from becoming a bespoke dev shop. Governed by `commercialization-doctrine-v1.md` §§4, 9.

> Canonical spellings: Hanzo, Enso, Lux, AMA Solutions, Zach Kelling, MAIM.

## 1. Default: Zach is not involved

The default for every rabbit engagement is **zero Zach involvement.** Diagnostics, standardized vaults, governance audits, and self-serve onboarding must be deliverable by AMA analysts/architects from documented playbooks on the pre-configured stack. If an offer *cannot* be delivered without Zach, it is not yet a rabbit — it is an elephant, and is priced and staffed accordingly (offer-portfolio §1, §4).

## 2. Escalate to Zach only when ALL of these hold

1. The opportunity is **commercially qualified** (scorecard ≥ 65; at or above the $20K floor, or an AMA-approved strategic exception).
2. The client has **budget and decision authority**.
3. **Proprietary Hanzo capability materially affects the sale** — the deal turns on something only deep engineering can demonstrate or build.
4. The opportunity has **strategic platform value** or could become a significant **elephant or whale**.

Additionally escalate when technical architecture materially changes the commercial structure, or when a proprietary capability must be demonstrated to close.

If any of these is missing, do not escalate — solve it at the AMA layer or route the prospect to a lower-touch cohort.

## 3. Never escalate for

- Routine discovery or unqualified opportunities.
- Tier-1 support, billing questions, password resets, or self-serve troubleshooting (these are product bugs to fix, not founder tasks — cohort §5 guardrail).
- Standardized deployments that the playbook already covers.
- Prospects who "want to talk to the technical founder" before commercial qualification — a disqualifier signal, not an escalation trigger (icp-framework §5).

## 4. Escalation path

1. **AMA architect** attempts resolution from the playbook and the internal knowledge base.
2. If blocked and the §2 tests pass, **AMA lead** packages a concise escalation: qualified opportunity summary, dollar value, the specific proprietary capability at stake, and the exact decision or demonstration needed from Zach.
3. **Zach** engages only on that packaged, qualified request — not open-ended.
4. Every escalation is logged (what was asked, why it qualified, outcome) so recurring escalations become the next playbook entry or the next thing to productize.

## 5. Escalation as a productization signal

A recurring escalation is a defect in standardization, not a fact of life. If the same request reaches Zach twice, it becomes a backlog item to (a) document into the playbook, (b) build into the self-serve product, or (c) reclassify the offer's tier. The goal is a monotonically decreasing rate of founder involvement per engagement as cohorts scale.

## 6. Relationship to the humans

AMA (Major) remains the accountable commercial counterpart and the client's primary relationship throughout (organizational-positioning §8). Escalation brings Zach *into* a qualified deal for a bounded purpose; it never transfers ownership of the relationship. This protects the scarce resource and keeps the buyer anchored to AMA.
