# 90-Day Validation Plan

A staged, disciplined sequence to validate the commercialization system and open the pipeline — without over-promising acquisition. "First 1,000 customers" is a scale architecture, not a 90-day acquisition promise (Decision 12); this plan validates the *machine*, not a headcount.

> Canonical spellings: Hanzo, Enso, Lux, AMA Solutions, Zach Kelling, MAIM.

## Phase 0 — Prerequisites (must hold before selling)

- The governance foundation is merged (PR #1) and the strategy layer is in review (PR #2). ✔ in progress.
- The flagship delivery module (AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic) exists with Phase A / Phase B separated, and its `internal-dry-run.md` has been executed on AMA/Hanzo's own spend before any client.
- Barred claims are scrubbed from all outbound assets (claim-governance §3).

## Days 1–30 — Internal lockdown (do not sell)

Goal: get the house in order so delivery can run without Zach.

- **Stabilize Hanzo Cloud** — uptime, the CLI, basic Git/app deploy, DNS/hosting; capture evidence toward moving E1–E2 (evidence-registry) from Founder-Reported toward Verified.
- **Stress-test every standardized deployment workflow** so a junior architect can run it from the playbook (agency-trap avoidance; cohort §3 prerequisite).
- **Advance the Enso evidence package (DR-8)** — assemble whatever reproducible benchmark methodology, configs, and reproducibility instructions Zach can produce now. This is the path from "Founder-Reported" to "Verified" for A2–A5.
- **Resolve the Enso data-governance statement (DR-6)** in writing — required before any Phase B pilot can be sold, especially into regulated ICP-5 data.
- **Run the diagnostic internal dry run** end-to-end; fix what breaks.

Exit criteria: diagnostic deliverable metadata-only; standardized workflows playbook-executable; Enso data-governance statement drafted; no barred claims in assets.

## Days 31–60 — Launch the flagship rabbit (high-touch)

Goal: prove the diagnostic commercially and generate the first cash-flowing case studies.

- **Outbound to the ICP-1 CFO / VP-Eng network** at or above the $20K/mo floor (DR-4), segmented by geography, vertical, and triggering event ("invoice shock").
- **Sell Phase A ($12–20K)** as a bounded, read-only, metadata-only engagement. Honor the north star: a legitimate outcome is "no responsible pilot here."
- **Only where Phase A justifies it, and only if DR-6 is resolved, offer Phase B ($8–15K).**
- **Target the 40 high-touch cohort** as the funding-and-proof engine — not the whole number.
- **Record every objection** and feed it back into `icp-framework.md` and the delivery playbook.

Exit criteria: a handful of paid Phase A engagements delivered; at least one expansion into Phase B or an elephant; objection library updated; first case study drafted (naming gated on DR-7).

## Days 61–90 — Standardize and open the funnel

Goal: shift from bespoke to repeatable, and turn on lower-touch cohorts.

- **Standardize the delivered offers** into playbooks for the 160 assisted cohort (junior-led, zero custom Hanzo code).
- **Open the self-serve CLI / API** to developer communities to begin the 500 self-serve cohort — only once the frictionless-self-serve prerequisites (cohort §3) hold.
- **Recruit the first 1–2 channel partners** (MSP/agency) to seed the 300 channel cohort; AMA supports the partner, not their clients.
- **Convert the first case study** into a repeatable sales asset (post-permission, DR-7).

Exit criteria: at least one standardized assisted delivery completed from the playbook; self-serve open with automated billing; first channel partner in motion; a repeatable case study asset.

## What this plan deliberately excludes

Per the strategic priorities (Sovereign OS Blueprint, "Immediate Strategic Priorities"), the following are **not** in the 90-day window: satellites, patent enforcement, national/critical-infrastructure deployments, and bringing Lux to the foreground. They are strategic context (doctrine §3); Lux stays background until a stable base exists.

## Success is the validated machine, not a number

The plan succeeds if, at day 90, AMA has: a diagnostic that delivers value even when the answer is "no"; delivery that runs without Zach; the beginnings of an Enso evidence package; a resolved data-governance statement; and repeatable playbooks for the assisted, channel, and self-serve cohorts. Acquiring literal customers is downstream of that machine working — not a promise of this window.
