# Decision Register

Records (1) the standing Current Decisions, (2) contradictions preserved across sources, and (3) new decisions with owners and dates. Contradictions are kept open on purpose — they are not silently resolved.

## Part 1 — Current Decisions (standing)

1. AMA is the capability architecture, commercialization and client-relationship layer.
2. Hanzo is the sovereign operating system and technical delivery foundation.
3. Enso is the intelligence-selection, routing and consensus layer — not "another model."
4. Lux provides coordination, provenance, ownership and settlement infrastructure.
5. The first commercial priority is rabbit-sized entry offers.
6. The flagship is the two-phase **AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic** plus **optional Enso Evaluation Pilot** — priced as two separate contracts (see Part 3, DR-3).
7. No savings percentages, benchmark supremacy or performance guarantees may be marketed without evidence.
8. Claims must use exactly one evidence status: Verified, Demonstrated, Founder-Reported, Roadmap, or Unknown / Requires Verification. Publisher and provenance — including Hanzo-Published — must be recorded separately. (See `docs/claim-governance.md`.)
9. Delivery must be designed to minimize Zach dependence (a design goal reached after dry-run, playbook and escalation validation — not a claim of current readiness).
10. Agent-operated companies must always be described as operating under human governance.
11. The initial active portfolio is: AI & Cloud Cost Diagnostic · AI Governance & Data Exposure Audit · Private Knowledge Vault / Firm Intelligence · Agentic Engineering Readiness Sprint (selective elephant-building wedge).
12. "First 1,000 customers" is a scale architecture, not a guaranteed 90-day acquisition promise.
13. Canonical names are Hanzo, Enso, Lux, AMA Solutions, Zach Kelling and MAIM.
